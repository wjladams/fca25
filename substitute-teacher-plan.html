Substitute Teacher Plan

Flexible plan for class periods when the teacher is unavailable.

Overview

This plan is designed to run smoothly with flexible timing. Aim for engagement, collaboration, and clear thinking. Encourage students to explain their reasoning out loud and on the board.

Total time is adjustable. If students are enthusiastic about an activity, allow it to continue a bit longer, then transition.

Part 1 — The Date Game (start of class)

Start with the Date Game: students use the digits of today’s date (in order) to create a true equation with exactly one equals sign. Try to find at least 2–3 equations before moving on.

Instructions and examples: Date Game page

Encourage students to work on it themselves and present their solution, either by writing it on the board or sharing it with the class and you writing it on the board.

Helpful Hints for the Date Game

  • The last two digits 2 and 5 can multiply to make 10. Try to build 10 out of the remaining digits and balance both sides.
  • The digits 2 and 0 from 2025 can multiply to make 0, so you can use an idea like 2 × 0 + 2 × 5 = 10 and then try to make 10 out of the month and day.
  • Multiplying by 0 zeroes a side quickly. For example, 2 × 0 × (2 + 5) makes 0 on that side. If the other side has a 0 in it, you can multiply that 0 by everything else to get 0 too.
  • Try out ideas for the left and right sides and work inward. Adjust operations to balance both sides.
  • Not all ideas work out—don’t get discouraged. Revise and try again.

Part 2 — Wordshake (after the Date Game)

Move on to playing wordshake.com. Students can play individually or in pairs.

Read about how Wordshake builds mathematical thinking: Wordshake & Mathematical Thinking.

  • Run a few short rounds (2–3 minutes each).
  • After a round, ask volunteers to share one high‑value word and how they found it.
  • Optionally, challenge students to beat their previous score in the next round.

Timing Guidance (adjust as needed)

  1. Date Game: 5–15 minutes (find 2–3 valid equations).
  2. Wordshake: the rest of the class period.
  3. Play multiple rounds of Wordshake if they tire of the first board.
